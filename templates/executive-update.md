# Executive Update Template

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Slides:** 8-12
**Audience:** Directors, VPs, C-level, senior leadership

Use this template when you are presenting to leadership. Executives have limited time and strong opinions. Lead with the bottom line, support it with data, and end with a clear ask.

---

## Structure

### Slide 1: Title and Context (30 seconds)

- Project or initiative name
- Date and reporting period
- Your name and role
- One-line summary: what this update is about

---

### Slide 2: Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF) (1.5 minutes)

- State the headline: are things on track, at risk, or off track?
- Use a clear status indicator: green/yellow/red or equivalent
- 2-3 bullet points covering the most important things leadership needs to know
- If there is a decision needed, state it here. Do not bury it on slide 9.

**Speaker note:** Executives often get pulled out of meetings early. If they leave after this slide, they should have what they need.

---

### Slide 3: Key Metrics (2 minutes)

- 3-4 metrics that tell the story
- Show trends, not just snapshots: "up 15% from last quarter"
- Compare against targets or benchmarks
- Use charts, not tables. Make the direction obvious at a glance.

---

### Slide 4: Progress Since Last Update (2 minutes)

- What was accomplished in the reporting period
- 3-4 bullet points, each tied to a deliverable or milestone
- Use specific language: "Shipped X" or "Completed Y" not "Made progress on Z"

---

### Slide 5: What Is At Risk (2 minutes)

- Be direct about blockers and risks
- For each risk: what it is, the impact if unresolved, and what you are doing about it
- 2-3 risks maximum. Prioritize by impact.
- If there are no risks, say so and move on. Do not invent filler.

---

### Slide 6: Resource or Decision Ask (1.5 minutes)

- If you need something from leadership, this is the slide
- Frame it clearly: "We need X to achieve Y by Z date"
- Provide options if there are multiple paths forward
- Make it easy to say yes. Pre-answer the obvious objections.

---

### Slide 7: Next Steps and Timeline (1.5 minutes)

- What happens in the next reporting period
- 3-4 concrete milestones with dates
- Who is responsible for what (if relevant at this level)

---

### Slide 8: Appendix / Backup (reference only)

- Detailed data, supporting charts, or technical details
- Do not present these unless asked
- Have them ready for the "Can you show me the numbers behind that?" question

---

## Tips for Executive Updates

- **Finish early.** Present for 10 minutes, not 15. Executives will fill the rest with the questions they actually care about. Let them.
- **Lead with the answer, not the journey.** State the conclusion first. Then support it. Do not build suspense; they do not want a mystery novel.
- **Data on every content slide.** Assertions without numbers get ignored. "We are making good progress" means nothing. "78% complete, on track for July 15" means something.
- **Kill the jargon.** If a term needs a glossary entry, use a different word. Every acronym is a speed bump.
- **Know your ask before you open your slide editor.** If you cannot finish the sentence "I need leadership to..." then you are not ready to build the deck.
- **Prepare for the redirect.** They will steer toward what they care about, not what you planned. Have backup slides and know how to jump to them without fumbling.
- **No surprises.** Bad news should reach their inbox before your slides reach the projector.
- **The 3-slide test:** If you could only show slides 1, 2, and 6, would leadership have what they need? If yes, the rest is supporting detail. If no, restructure.

## Calibration: What Good Looks Like

**Bad exec slide:**

> **Progress Update**
> - Made progress on migration
> - Working through remaining issues
> - Team is heads down on delivery
> - Will provide update next quarter

Every bullet here could apply to any project in the company. Leadership learns nothing. They lose trust because it looks like you are hiding behind vague language.

**Good exec slide:**

> **Migration: 78% complete. On track for July 15.**
> [Progress bar: 78% filled, green status]
> - 312 of 400 services migrated (up from 260 last month)
> - 3 blockers remain: auth service (fix shipping June 30), data pipeline (owner: SRE team), legacy API (decision needed from this group)
> - Ask: approve deprecation of legacy API by July 1

Specific numbers. Clear status. One ask with a date. The exec can make a decision in 30 seconds.
