# Ignite Talk Template

Exactly 20 slides, 15 seconds each, auto-advancing. Total time: 5 minutes. No mercy.

This is the hardest format in public speaking. You cannot pause, you cannot go back, you cannot skip ahead. The slides advance whether you are ready or not.

**Duration:** 5 minutes (exactly)
**Slides:** 20 (exactly, auto-advancing every 15 seconds)
**Audience:** Conference Ignite sessions, unconference lightning rounds

## Ignite Survival Guide

### Scripting
- Write your script word for word. This is the ONE format where scripting is not just acceptable, it is required.
- Each slide gets exactly 2-3 sentences. No more. Count them.
- Total script length: roughly 750-900 words for the full 5 minutes.

### Slide Design
- Use only images and single lines of text on slides
- No bullet points anywhere
- High-contrast visuals that read from the back of the room
- Dark backgrounds with light text, or bold images with minimal overlay

### Practice
- Practice with a 15-second timer until you can hit every transition without thinking
- Record yourself and watch it. You will talk faster than you think.
- Have a friend watch and signal if you are out of sync
- Practice at least 10 times. This is not an exaggeration.
- Practice standing up, out loud, with the slides running on auto-advance

### During the Talk
- If you fall behind, skip to your next slide's script. Do not try to catch up.
- If you get ahead, slow down and add a pause. Silence is fine for 3 seconds.
- The audience forgives imperfection in Ignite. They do not forgive going over time (the slides literally will not let you).
- Humor works well in Ignite because the format itself is funny: you against the clock.
- Breathe. Your adrenaline will spike. That is normal.

### Common Mistakes
- Trying to fit a 30-minute talk into 5 minutes (pick one idea, not five)
- Using bullet-point slides (the audience cannot read them in 15 seconds)
- Winging it (you will crash by slide 8)
- Starting with "Hi, my name is..." (the MC already introduced you; use those 15 seconds)
- Ending with "I had more slides but ran out of time" (you have exactly the right number of slides)
