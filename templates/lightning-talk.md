# Lightning Talk Template

**Duration:** 5 minutes
**Slides:** 5-7
**Audience:** Varies (conference, meetup, internal all-hands)

Use this template when you have 5 minutes to make one point and make it stick. No warmup, no filler, no "about me" slide. Every second counts.

---

## Structure

### Slide 1: The Hook (30 seconds)

- Open with a surprising stat, a bold claim, or a question
- No agenda slide. No introductions. Start with the thing that matters.
- The audience decides in the first 15 seconds whether to pay attention. Win those 15 seconds.

**Examples of strong hooks:**
- "We cut our deploy time from 45 minutes to 90 seconds. Here is how."
- "Your monitoring is lying to you. Let me show you why."
- "Raise your hand if you have been paged at 2 AM for a problem that fixed itself."

---

### Slide 2: The Problem (1 minute)

- What is broken, missing, or painful?
- One concrete example the audience recognizes
- 2 bullet points maximum

---

### Slide 3: The Insight (1 minute)

- The key idea, approach, or realization
- This is the core of your talk. Spend the most energy here.
- One visual or diagram that makes the idea click

---

### Slide 4: The Evidence (1 minute)

- Show that it works: a result, a metric, a before/after comparison
- One compelling data point beats three mediocre ones
- Screenshot or demo output if applicable

---

### Slide 5: The Takeaway (1 minute)

- Restate the main point in one sentence
- Tell the audience exactly what to do next: "Try X," "Read Y," "Stop doing Z"
- QR code or short link to resources

---

### Slide 6 (optional): Resources (30 seconds)

- Only if you have a repo, blog post, or tool to share
- QR code is mandatory for lightning talks; nobody is writing down URLs

---

## Tips for Lightning Talks

- **Practice with a timer.** If your rehearsal runs 5:30, you are over. Cut a slide.
- **One idea only.** If you cannot state your talk's point in one sentence, it is not focused enough.
- **No "about me" slide.** The MC introduced you. Use that time for content.
- **No Q&A.** There is no time. Put your contact info on the last slide and say "Find me after."
- **Speak faster than you think you should.** Lightning talk pacing feels rushed in rehearsal but sounds energetic on stage.
- **End on time or early.** Finishing at 4:30 is a power move. Going to 5:30 is rude.
- **The single test:** After your talk, can an audience member turn to their neighbor and explain your point in one sentence? If not, simplify.
