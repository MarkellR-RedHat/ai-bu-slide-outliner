# Panel Preparation Template

Preparation guide for participating in a panel discussion. No slides; all talking points and positions.

**Duration:** 30-60 minutes (your speaking time: 8-15 minutes total)
**Format:** Talking points and positions, not slides
**Audience:** Varies by panel topic

---

## Your Position Statement (2 minutes max)

The moderator will ask each panelist to introduce their perspective. This is your one prepared moment.

**Structure:** "I believe [position] because [evidence]. The most important thing to understand is [key insight]."

Practice this to exactly 90 seconds. You will be cut off at 2 minutes.

**Draft your statement here:**

> [Your position statement]

## Bridge Phrases

Stock phrases to redirect from a weak question to your message:

- "That is an interesting question, and it connects to something I think is even more important..."
- "I would frame it differently..."
- "The real question behind that question is..."
- "Let me give you a concrete example of why that matters..."
- "I have seen this play out in practice, and here is what actually happens..."

---

## Landmines to Avoid

### Topics You Should Not Weigh In On
- Competitors (by name)
- Unannounced features or roadmap items
- Internal politics or organizational disputes
- Anything you do not have direct experience with

### Trap Questions to Watch For
- Questions designed to get you to commit to something ("So you are saying X will never work?")
- Hypotheticals meant to create headlines ("Would you say that Y is dead?")
- Overly broad questions that invite a wrong answer ("Is open source always better?")

### How to Gracefully Decline
- "That is outside my area, but I can tell you what I do know about..."
- "I would not want to speculate on that without better data."
- "That deserves a more nuanced answer than I can give in 30 seconds."

---

## Panel Etiquette Tips

- Do not interrupt, but do interject when you have a strong point ("Can I build on that?")
- Reference other panelists' points to show you are listening ("Building on what [name] said...")
- If you are losing the audience, tell a short story
- Keep answers under 60 seconds unless the moderator invites you to expand
- Do not read notes on stage, but have them on your phone or a card in your pocket
- Make eye contact with the audience, not just the moderator
- Nod when other panelists make good points; it reads well on camera
- If another panelist says something wrong, correct gently: "My experience has been different..."
